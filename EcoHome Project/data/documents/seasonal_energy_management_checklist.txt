Seasonal Energy Management Checklist

Energy strategy should change with the season because weather, daylight, and household routines affect both consumption and solar generation. A simple seasonal checklist keeps the home efficient year-round.

In summer, focus on cooling loads and solar heat gain. Close blinds during the hottest afternoon hours, use ceiling fans to reduce air-conditioning demand, and pre-cool the home during cheaper morning hours. Clean HVAC filters and confirm attic ventilation is working.

In winter, reduce heat loss first. Seal drafts around doors and windows, verify insulation performance, and use programmable setbacks during sleep or away periods. If electric resistance heating is present, avoid peak-rate heating whenever possible.

During spring and fall shoulder seasons, take advantage of mild outdoor temperatures. Use natural ventilation when humidity allows, reduce unnecessary HVAC runtime, and schedule maintenance before extreme weather returns.

Seasonal planning should also include solar and storage adjustments. Summer usually supports higher solar self-consumption, while winter may require more grid support and tighter off-peak scheduling. Review bills and device runtimes at the start of each season to update operating rules and savings goals.
